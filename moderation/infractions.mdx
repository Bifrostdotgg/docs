Every moderation action in Heimdall — whether issued by a staff member or triggered by an automod rule — creates an infraction record. The infraction history gives staff a complete picture of any member's moderation record, including the current active point total.

</Frame>

## Viewing a member's infractions

### Via slash command

```text
/infractions <user>
```

Displays the member's infraction history directly in Discord. The response lists each infraction with its type, reason, point value, issuing staff member or rule, and whether the points are currently active or expired.

### Via the dashboard

Navigate to **Moderation \> Infractions** in the dashboard. Use the search bar to find a member by username or ID. Click on a member to open their full record.

The dashboard view includes:

- **Total active points** — the current point total contributing to escalation thresholds
- **Total infractions** — lifetime count of all infraction records
- **Infraction timeline** — each individual record with full detail

## Managing infractions

From the dashboard, staff with the appropriate permissions can:

- **Expire an infraction manually** — removes the points from the member's total immediately without waiting for the natural expiry date
- **Delete an infraction** — permanently removes the record (use for mistakes or false positives)
- **Edit an infraction** — update the reason or point value on an existing record

## Setting points directly

```text
/setpoints <user> <points>
```

Sets a member's active infraction point total to a specific value, bypassing the individual infraction record. This is useful for manually adjusting a member's position within the escalation system without creating additional infraction records.

## Dashboard filtering

The Infractions dashboard page supports filtering by:

- Date range
- Action type (warn, ban, kick, mute, automod)
- Staff member who issued the action
- Whether the infraction is active or expired

## Required permissions

| Action | Permission key |
| --- | --- |
| Viewing infraction history | `moderation.config.view_config` |
| Editing and expiring infractions | `moderation.manageRules` |
